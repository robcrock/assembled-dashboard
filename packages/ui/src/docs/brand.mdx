## The deviations register

Every departure from the spec is deliberate, and this is its record:

1. **AA-solved inks.** Secondary `#6B6F74` measures 4.02:1 as body text on the
   spec's own page; tertiary `#D95600` measures 3.15:1. Both stay in the ramps as
   fill/large-type values; *text* inks use solved steps (concrete-600 at 4.70:1,
   orange-700 at 4.70:1, orange-400 in dark). Accessibility outranks literalism.
2. **Amber stays.** The spec's single-accent rule meets a triage dashboard that
   needs a middle register between quiet and alarm: the urgency ramp is
   grey â†’ amber â†’ orange (at-risk ink is amber-800, minted for AA on cream). One
   documented deviation, paid for scanning speed.
3. **Borders are quiet.** The spec uses secondary-strength grid strokes; six data
   surfaces ruled that loudly would out-shout the data. Hairlines are concrete-200.
4. **Dark is authored.** The spec ships no dark theme; ours inverts the concrete
   ramp (#0F1113 page, concrete-900 cards, cream inks, orange constant in meaning).
   Invented values, flagged as such.
5. **Radius residuals.** A few curves are geometry, not token: the meter and
   deviation-bar `rounded-full` tracks, the gauge's round linecaps, the monogram
   tile.
6. **Stock destructive tints sit at 4.09:1.** The vendored Badge/Button
   `destructive` variants pair the breach ink with a 10% tint of itself, which
   lands under AA over the page concrete (axe: 4.09 vs 4.5). No consumer ships
   them â€” the system's own breach surfaces (StatusBadge, breach-ink values) are
   AA-solved on their real surfaces â€” and the variants stay stock for upstream
   fidelity rather than being forked or re-tinted. Known, bounded, unshipped.
7. **Sentiment reserves are off-spec.** Red `#C0392B` and teal `#5F9EA0` come from
   a retro-industrial inspiration palette, not `DESIGN.md`. They exist as full
   primitive ramps plus reserved semantic tokens (`--sentiment-*`) and are
   consumed by nothing (see the color law's sentiment register). Copper `#B87333`
   and olive `#7D8B3F` from the same palette were rejected â€” four chromatic hues
   is the budget's ceiling. Its orange `#FF6037` was considered and rejected as an
   accent swap: `#D95600` stays the one alarm.

## How to re-brand

The procedure this layer proved: swap the primitive ramps and the font pair in
`globals.css` + font loading, keep every semantic role name, re-run the AA solve
for text inks (the canvas-readback check lives in the PR history), and touch no
component. If a re-brand *needs* a component edit, that component has a token leak
â€” fix the leak, not the brand.
